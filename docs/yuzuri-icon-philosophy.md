# Yuzuri Icon Design Philosophy — "Still Inheritance"

## Form and Essence

The philosophy of *Still Inheritance* centers on the act of quiet transmission — the way living things pass their form, knowledge, and care to what comes after. Visual language is drawn from botanical observation: the precise geometry of a single leaf vein, the way cellular structure repeats at every scale, the dignified stillness of something preserved and cherished. Form is never decorative. Every curve and line exists because life made it necessary.

## Space and Material

Negative space is treated as active matter. The background is not emptiness — it is the depth of evening moss, the interior of old lacquer, a green so saturated it becomes a place rather than a color. Shapes float with meticulous calibration, surrounded by breathing room that gives each element the gravity of something set down with intention. The surface should feel like paper made from pressed leaves: fine-grained, cool, permanent.

## Color and Luminosity

A palette of deep teal-green (#1a4a3a to #2d6b54) graduates toward cooler shadow at the periphery and warms almost imperceptibly at the center, as though a single light source — far away, steady, natural — illuminates from within. White is reserved as the material of the drawn line itself: pure, unmodified, carrying no warmth or coolness. The contrast is severe and intentional — the kind that can be read from great distance and in old age.

## Rhythm and Repetition

A single compositional gesture is made with absolute confidence and never repeated. The leaf form is drawn once, with botanical precision but no scientific coldness — the tip curves with the slight asymmetry of a real specimen, the central vein carries subsidiary lines that thin to hairline at their ends. A rectangular form within the leaf represents the page: open, unmarked, waiting. The painstaking calibration of weight between leaf and page — their proportional relationship, their shared axis — must feel inevitable, as though no other arrangement was ever possible.

## Craftsmanship

Every vector point is placed as if the file will be printed at three meters and scrutinized at centimeter range. The icon is to be executed with the same reverence applied to a woodblock printer's final proof: no hesitation, no revision visible in the result, only the clean evidence of deep expertise. Stroke weights are resolved to the sub-pixel; the gradient transitions are tuned so they read identically on OLED and LCD. This is the work of someone who has made ten thousand marks to earn the right to make this one.
